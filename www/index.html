Strengthening Hyper-Local Government

ANCBrigade is a project in Washington, D.C. to strengthen our unique hyper-local level of government called Advisory Neighborhood Commissions (ANCs).

The District is divided into 40 ANCs, about 5 per ward. Each ANC is made up of about 7-8 commissioners. Commissioners are each elected from Single Member Districts (SMDs) comprising about 2,000 District residents. There are 296 ANC commissioners (and SMDs) in the District.

This website is a project of the DC chapter of the Code for America Brigade. We’re volunteers interested in using technology to solve problems in our civic lives. This is a work in progress.

What Are ANCs?

Washington DC is divided into 40 districts called Advisory Neighborhood Commissions, or ANCs. Each ANC has 7-8 ANC commissioners, who are your most local government representative in DC.

ANCs hold regular meetings and make decisions about a range of issues, including traffic, parking, recreation, street improvements, liquor licenses, zoning, economic development, police protection, sanitation and trash collection, and the District's annual budget. Learn more about ANC authority.

Each ANC is divided into 7-8 Single Member Districts, or SMDs. Commissioners are each elected from Single Member Districts, and they each represent about 2,000 DC residents. There are 296 ANC commissioners (and SMDs) in the District.

You elect your ANC Commissioner every two years. They do not receive a salary, but they receive grant money for the general purpose of improving their area and hiring staff. Learn more about ANC grant money.

Why should I care about my ANC?

In addition to awarding grant money as they see fit, ANC Commissioners’ main power comes from making decisions about neighborhood issues and then advising the DC Council and DC government agencies to take action - and this is nearly always the final decision in a matter.

Goals

Our goals are evolving as we get to know the ANC system better. Here are the goals we are considering:

  • Educate the public on DC’s unique ANC system.
  • Provide tools for ANC commissioners to connect with their constituents and to more easily publish official ANC records.
  • Help DC residents locate their ANC, its meetings, and the commissioner from their SMD.
  • Inspire DC residents to be involved in the ANC system.
  • Promote transparency and accountability bylaw recommendations.
